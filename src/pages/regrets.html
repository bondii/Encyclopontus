A phrase I've heard often, especially during my teenage years, is
Have no regrets - the bad experiences are part of who you are today!

While I do agree with the meaning, I think this should be switched around, and that instead of having no regrets we should honor them:

It's not the experience of doing something I later regret that has made me who I am;
It is he feeling of regret that has made me change my behavior.

Right around when I turned 15 I got into my first relationship. I was overwhelmed and inexperienced, as was she: in hindsight it was a toxic relationship for both parts, and I certainly played my role.

Some parts were fantastic and I learned a LOT from positive moments and interactions with her, but I also have lots of regrets from that time; for how I treated myself, her, and others in my life. A big part of the problem was my lack of prior experiences, and I remember that I from the start thought:

This is my major chance of having a girlfriend, I need to make this work!
...Not a great base for a healthy relationship built on constructive feedback and mutual respect..

A year or two before this I had gotten contact with a friend's friend, who I had been texting with and subsequently had a crush on. This wasn't just "oh a girl, I'm in love!!", though: I really enjoyed texting with her, her inputs on questions, her humour, everything about her, I honestly was kind of in love... If only I had told her how I felt, I think there's at least a chance she would have had some feelings for me as well, or maybe they could have flowered, but I had never had a girlfriend at this point and my Initiative was still at Zer0. Insecure as I was, I was completely sure I was just a lunatic boy with strong feelings that she would never reciprocate, so I didn't want to risk ruining our friendship. Hence, nothing happened.

Fast-forward a year or two again and I was in my first relationship. I had told her about how I had had feelings for this girl previously (all while lying about said feelings being complete gone), and she did not like me spending time texting her, especially when I was hanging out with my girlfriend. Mind you, this was at a time when SMSing was the thang: I texted people almost 24/7, even when hanging out with others. Still, I can see why it was not appreciated that I spent my time texting this other girl, whom I had admitted to ("previously"ðŸ™„) having feelings for. My GF brought it up a couple of times, which always led to a fight as I reasoned there was no harm done, while our communication was not good enough to make her feel heard and assured. I was a huuge wimp with a fear of conflicts (read: no relationship experience), so eventually I bulged and simply never responded to this other girl.

I ghosted her

For many years I blamed my (then) GF for making me do this. However with the years and some distance from it, it was clearly 100% my decision: she did not ask me to ghost her, and when asked about it later I lied that "we lost contact".

I ghosted who I just a year before considered almost my best friend, and who probably could have been.

I am the one responsible what I did

This is without a doubt the single greatest regret of my life, and I value this as one of the most influential life lessons I have had.
