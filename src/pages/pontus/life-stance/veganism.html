Without going into details, I think the way animals are treated in the modern food industry is one of the darkest and most shameful parts of humans throughout history.

I believe one of the most human attributes is the ability to make choices based on rational reasoning.
I also personally believe one of humankind's goals (as well as each individual's) should be to reduce suffering in the world.

These two together with (based on what I've read and experienced) the fact that it is not only possible but actually quite easy to to live a healthy happy life on a plant-based diet, I don't see any option but to.

I have tried but think it's very hard to try and talk about this on my own initiative. Even if my conscious motivation is a need to share thoughts and deeper feelings in order to connect with others, I realize my feelings on this subject are so strong that it's practically impossible to hold a neutral "I don't care what you do, but this is how I think" tone when talking about it, and always tend to heavily imply that I think it is morally non-optional, even if that's not how I want to express myself. This naturally easily leads to the listener falling into more of a defensive stance. Hence, I try to be an example of that it is indeed possible, but these days almost exclusively talk about it when asked.

With such a deeply societally rooted situation, I don't think change will come (fast enough) without being as absolute as feels personally reasonable. Ultimately I do care about change happening, and I feel each and every participation in the animals-as-food world is a continued normalization of what I consider ethically unreasonable.
